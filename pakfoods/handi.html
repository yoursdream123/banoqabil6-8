Chicken Handi

Chicken handi is a quintessential dish in South Asian cuisine, celebrated for its rich and aromatic flavors. This culinary masterpiece involves tender chicken pieces cooked in a luscious, creamy gravy, infused with a medley of spices that tantalize the taste buds. The dish gets its name from the traditional handi, a deep, narrow-mouthed cooking vessel, where the magic unfolds. The combination of yogurt, tomatoes, onions, and an assortment of spices like cumin, coriander, and garam masala creates a harmonious blend that coats the succulent chicken, resulting in a dish that's both comforting and indulgent. Garnished with fresh coriander leaves and served alongside warm naan or fragrant rice, chicken handi is a delightful treat that embodies the essence of aromatic and flavorful Pakistani and Indian cuisine.

Certainly! Chicken Handi is a creamy and flavorful Pakistani dish cooked in a traditional handi (clay pot) or a deep pan. Here’s a classic recipe for Chicken Handi:

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg chicken pieces, preferably bone-in
  • 3-4 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 2-3 onions, finely sliced
  • 2-3 tomatoes, finely chopped or blended
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon green chili paste (adjust according to spice preference)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 1/2 cup fresh cream
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Ginger julienne for garnish
  • Green chilies for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a handi or deep pan over medium heat.
  2. Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle for a few seconds.
  3. Add sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a couple of minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add chicken pieces to the handi and fry them on medium-high heat until they change color and any water released from the chicken evaporates.
  6. Once the chicken is lightly browned, add chopped or blended tomatoes. Cook until they soften and blend well with the chicken.
  7. Add green chili paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, and salt to taste. Mix well to coat the chicken with the spices.
  8. Cover the handi with a lid and let the chicken cook for about 15-20 minutes on low to medium heat, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.
  9. In a separate bowl, whisk the yogurt until smooth and add it to the handi. Mix well.
  10. Cook for another 5-10 minutes until the chicken is fully cooked and tender.
  11. Add fresh cream and garam masala powder. Stir gently to combine all the ingredients.
  12. Simmer for a few more minutes until the curry thickens slightly and the flavors meld together.
  13. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves, ginger julienne, and green chilies before serving.

Serve hot with naan bread or steamed rice.

Chicken Handi pairs wonderfully with naan, roti, or steamed rice. Adjust the spice levels to suit your taste preferences, and enjoy this creamy and aromatic Pakistani dish!
