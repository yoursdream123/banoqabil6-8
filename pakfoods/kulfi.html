Kulfi

whole milk, condensed milk, and a touch of cream, its preparation is a labor of love, slowly reducing on stovetops, intensifying flavors into a rich, velvety base. Fragrant with cardamom and often adorned with saffron, each spoonful carries the nostalgic aroma of distant spice bazaars. Laden with chopped nuts—pistachios, almonds, and cashews—its texture boasts a delightful crunch amidst the smoothness.

Pakistani kulfi is a delightful frozen dessert with a rich, creamy texture. Here's a recipe for making it at home:

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups whole milk
  • 1 cup condensed milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup chopped nuts (pistachios, almonds, cashews)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • Saffron strands (optional)
  • Kulfi molds or small cups for freezing
  • Popsicle sticks or skewers (if using cups)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Milk Mixture:
    • In a heavy-bottomed pan, pour the whole milk and bring it to a boil on medium heat. Stir frequently to prevent burning.
    • Once it starts boiling, reduce the heat to low-medium and let it simmer. Stir occasionally and let the milk reduce to almost half its original quantity. This can take about 30-40 minutes.
  2. Add Condensed Milk and Sugar:
    • Once the milk has thickened, add the condensed milk and sugar. Stir well until the sugar dissolves completely.
    • Allow the mixture to simmer for another 10-15 minutes on low heat.
  3. Add Cream and Flavorings:
    • Pour in the heavy cream and mix it thoroughly with the milk mixture.
    • Add cardamom powder and saffron strands (if using) for flavor. Stir well to combine.
  4. Mix in Nuts:
    • Add the chopped nuts into the mixture and stir until evenly distributed.
  5. Cooling and Freezing:
    • Let the mixture cool down to room temperature.
    • Pour the cooled mixture into kulfi molds or small cups.
    • If using cups, cover each cup with aluminum foil and insert a popsicle stick or skewer in the center of each cup.
  6. Freeze:
    • Place the molds or cups in the freezer for at least 6-8 hours, preferably overnight, until the kulfi is completely set.
  7. Serve:
    • To serve, take the molds or cups out of the freezer.
    • If using molds, briefly dip them in lukewarm water to loosen the kulfi. Gently pull out the kulfi from the molds.
    • If using cups, remove the foil and slide the kulfi out.
    • Serve the delicious Pakistani kulfi slices or popsicles on a plate or stick for enjoyment!

Remember, kulfi is wonderfully versatile, so feel free to adjust the sweetness, flavorings, or nuts according to your taste preferences. Enjoy your homemade Pakistani kulfi!
