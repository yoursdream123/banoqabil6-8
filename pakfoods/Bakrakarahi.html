Bakra Karahi

Bakra Karahi, a quintessential dish in Pakistani cuisine, embodies the essence of indulgence and communal dining. This aromatic preparation revolves around tender goat or lamb meat, cooked in a large, rounded karahi (wok) with a medley of spices that infuse the dish with a rich, robust flavor. The succulent pieces of meat, simmered slowly to perfection, are complemented by a tantalizing blend of tomatoes, ginger, garlic, and a symphony of aromatic spices like cumin, coriander, and chili. What makes Bakra Karahi a standout dish is not just its taste but also the communal aspect of enjoying it with friends and family. Served steaming hot, straight from the karahi, it's shared among diners who tear into soft naan bread or chapatis to savor every morsel. This dish encapsulates the warmth of Pakistani hospitality and the exquisite flavors that make it a celebrated part of the country's culinary heritage.

Pakistani Bakra Karahi is a popular dish made with goat meat cooked in a karahi (wok-like pan) with aromatic spices. Here's a traditional recipe for you:

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg goat meat (preferably with bones), cut into pieces
  • 1/2 cup oil or ghee
  • 2-3 medium-sized onions, finely sliced
  • 3-4 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon green chili paste (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Ginger julienne for garnish
  • Green chilies for garnish
  • Lemon wedges

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a large karahi or deep pan over medium-high heat.
  2. Add sliced onions and saut√© until they turn golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a few minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add goat meat pieces to the karahi and fry them on high heat until they change color and the water released from the meat evaporates.
  5. Once the meat is browned, add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and blend in with the meat.
  6. Add green chili paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt. Mix well to coat the meat evenly with the spices.
  7. Cover the karahi with a lid and let the meat cook on low to medium heat for about 30-40 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking and ensure even cooking.
  8. Check the tenderness of the meat. If it's not yet tender, continue cooking until it reaches the desired texture.
  9. Once the meat is tender, increase the heat to evaporate any excess liquid, ensuring the consistency is thick and the oil separates from the masala.
  10. Sprinkle garam masala over the dish and mix well.
  11. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves, ginger julienne, green chilies, and lemon wedges before serving.

Serve hot with naan bread or steamed rice.

Bakra Karahi is often enjoyed with naan bread, roti, or steamed rice. Adjust the spice levels according to your taste preferences, and enjoy this flavorful Pakistani dish!
