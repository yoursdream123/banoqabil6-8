Chicken Karahi

Chicken karahi is a beloved Pakistani dish celebrated for its vibrant flavors and robust spices. Cooked in a traditional karahi (wok-like cooking vessel), this dish bursts with the aromatic symphony of tomatoes, ginger, garlic, and a melange of spices like cumin, coriander, and chili. The succulent chicken, simmered in its juices along with the fragrant spices, takes on a tender texture that melts in the mouth. The dish embodies a perfect balance between heat and tanginess, heightened by the fresh cilantro sprinkled on top. Whether relished with fluffy naan or aromatic basmati rice, chicken karahi is a culinary journey through Pakistan's rich gastronomic heritage, showcasing the country's mastery of flavors and its cultural essence in every delightful bite.

Chicken karahi is a flavorful and popular Pakistani dish. Here's a basic recipe:

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces
  • 4 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup oil
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a karahi or a heavy-bottomed pan. Add cumin seeds and let them crackle.
  2. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.
  3. Add minced garlic and ginger. Sauté for a minute until the raw aroma disappears.
  4. Add chicken pieces and fry until they turn golden brown. Stir occasionally to ensure they cook evenly.
  5. Once the chicken changes color, add chopped tomatoes and green chilies. Cook until tomatoes soften and release their juices.
  6. Add red chili powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for a few minutes until the oil starts to separate from the masala.
  7. Cover the karahi and let the chicken cook in its own juices for about 15-20 minutes on medium-low heat. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  8. Once the chicken is cooked through, sprinkle garam masala and mix gently.
  9. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

Serve hot with naan bread or steamed rice.

This recipe is versatile, and variations exist based on personal preferences and regional styles. Adjust the spice levels and ingredients according to your taste. Enjoy your homemade chicken karahi!
